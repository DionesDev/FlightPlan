Reminders in filing a Flight Plan

When completing a flight plan the following rules apply.

  • 1. Use capital letters, one letter in each space of the field (unless field are not divided into spaces)
  • 2. Adhere to the prescribed formats and manner of specifying data
  • 3. Any data should be inserted only in the fields and spaces provided.
  • 4. Where excess space is available, leave unused spaces blank.
  • 5. All times should be clock times in 4 figures UTC (e.g., 1800, 0930, etc.)
  • 6. The term “aerodrome”, where used in the flight plan, is intended to cover also sites other than aerodromes, which may be used by certain types of aircraft, e.g., helicopters or balloons.
  • 7. Note that item numbers on the form are not consecutive (they correspond to item type in standard ATS messages).
  • 8. Items preceding item 7 are to be completed by ATC and COM services
  • 9. Items 7 to 18 should be completed as indicated hereunder.
  • 10. Item 19 completion is to facilitate alerting of SAR (Search and Rescue) services.

Welcome to AVC flight planning portal

AVC Flight Planner is a web-based flight planning tool tailored for Aviation Communications students at the Philippine State College of Aeronautics. Its purpose is to offer hands-on experience in flight planning, enrich aviation knowledge, and contribute to the aviation sector's progress. The software provides a user-friendly interface where users can input flight parameters, like departure and arrival airports, preferred routes, airspace constraints, and fuel considerations. Additionally, it furnishes educational resources such as tutorials and reference materials, cultivating a collaborative environment by allowing students to share and receive feedback on their flight plans from both peers and instructors.

In essence, AVC Flight Planner is a powerful tool designed to empower students in their aviation education journey. By engaging with this software, students not only acquire practical flight planning experience but also cultivate vital decision-making and critical thinking skills. This combination of hands-on application and collaborative learning helps students enhance their understanding of flight planning concepts while contributing to the advancement of the aviation industry.
